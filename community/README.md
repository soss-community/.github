# SOSS - Community

## Who we are

SOSS is a managed community for creating open source software that solves real problems or helps working/living.  
That means nothing changes without review, no code ships without intent, and membership is not automatic.

We are not a forum. We are not a Discord server.  
We are a small group of people who care about building software the right way.

---

## Transparency

We want to ensure everyone has the chance to see what we do.
No hidden agenda. No hidden data grabbing.

There is just one thing we won't be fully transparent about:  
The security internals of our code.
Not because we have something to hide, but because publishing attack surfaces would put our users at risk.
Everyone makes mistakes. We just don't want ours to become open doors.

## Our Mission

Free software. Free tools.

Why should we make money out of something that was originally built to help people?
Software started as a way to make work easier, life a little lighter, time a little longer.

**Time is the most crucial resource we have.**
No matter how rich you get, you can never get more of it.

We want to give some of that time back,  
or at least stop it from being wasted on things that repeat,  
follow a pattern, and could just as well be handled by code.

This is not about you specifically. It is about anyone.  
If our tools help just one person find enough time to grow,  
maybe that person builds something that helps all of us someday.

In short: we care.  
About you. About people we will never meet. About ourselves.

We will not tolerate behaviour that goes against this, including towards yourself.  
If you do not take care of your own health and mental state,  
you cannot create what you are truly capable of.

## Visible membership

Not everyone can become a visible community member.

To be listed as a member you need to meet a minimum standard across four areas:

**Ethics**
You understand why we build the way we build.  
Clean, no bloat, no ads, security first.  
You do not cut corners and you do not pretend you did not.

It's important to know your own limits! If you know someone knows more about a topic, ASK!  
Don't let your ego get in the way of creating good software.  
That's a sign of beeing conscientious, and a minimum requirement for real growth here!

**Security knowledge**
You understand the basics of secure software development.  
You do not need to be a security engineer, but you need to think about what could go wrong before you ship.  


**Communication**
You can explain your thinking clearly. We write only in English and our prefered language to speak in covnersations is English too.  
You do not need to be a native speaker, you need to be precise and honest.  
If you cant translate something, just explain the concept behind. Same as programming ;)  
In short, this is a point where you will allways grow fast in communities.

**People skills**
You can guide others without talking down to them. You know the difference between feedback and criticism. 
You treat everyone the same regardless of experience level.

If you meet these standards and want to get involved, just reach out. We will have a conversations.

## Code style

All SOSS code and documentation follows a shared style guide to guarantee consistency across projects.

Style documentation is maintained per project in its own repository.

General principles:
- Clarity over cleverness
- Explicit over implicit
- One purpose per function, one function per purpose
- Comments explain *why*, not only *what*

---

## How to get involved

You do not need to be a member to contribute ideas.

- Open an issue in the relevant repository
- Add code examples where helpful
- Be specific about the problem you are solving

If your contribution influences what we build, you will be credited — in the `CREDITS.md` of that project.
